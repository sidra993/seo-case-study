Executive Summary

A Manchester-based plumbing company with over 12 years of trading experience was generating zero online leads. Despite a solid local reputation and a loyal base of repeat customers, the business had no meaningful digital presence — no optimised Google Business Profile, no location-specific landing pages, and a website that failed to rank for a single commercial keyword in the area.

Competitors with inferior service were capturing every local search query. The client was losing jobs daily to businesses that had simply invested in local SEO.

Over a focused 90-day engagement, a full local SEO strategy was executed: Google Business Profile creation and optimisation, 45 local citations, five city-specific service pages, a complete technical SEO audit and remediation, Core Web Vitals improvements, and a structured internal linking architecture.

The results were measurable and rapid. Within 90 days, the business was generating 28 inbound leads per month, ranking on Page 1 for 14 high-intent local keywords, appearing in Google's Local 3-Pack for key search terms, and receiving consistent calls and enquiry form submissions directly from organic search.

Client Background

Who They Are

The client is a sole-trader plumbing business operating in Manchester and surrounding areas, with 12 years of continuous trading. Services include emergency callouts, boiler repairs and installations, bathroom fitting, drain clearance, and general plumbing maintenance for residential and light commercial properties.

Their Position Before SEO

Despite over a decade of consistent work, the business had never invested in digital marketing. Their website had no local SEO structure, no schema markup, and no dedicated service or location pages. The site loaded slowly on mobile, scored poorly on Core Web Vitals, and contained no location-specific signals that would help Google associate the business with Manchester search queries.

The Google Business Profile was either unclaimed or only partially set up, with no photos, no services listed, no description, and fewer than five reviews visible. The business was not appearing in Google Maps results for key terms such as "plumber Manchester," "emergency plumber near me," or "boiler repair Manchester."

Business Goals

The client's primary objective was to generate inbound enquiries from local customers searching for plumbing services online, and to establish a sustainable long-term search presence that reduced reliance on word-of-mouth.

SEO Challenges

01
No Google Business Profile Presence

The GBP was either unclaimed or sparsely populated — no photos, categories, services, or description.

02
Zero Local Keyword Rankings

The website did not appear in any tracked results for commercial local queries. Competitors were occupying all visible Page 1 positions.

03
No Location or Service Landing Pages

The site lacked dedicated pages for each service or city, making it structurally impossible to rank for most target keywords.

04
Inconsistent NAP Data Across the Web

Business name, address, and phone number were inconsistent or missing across directories, suppressing local ranking trust signals.

05
Poor Technical SEO Foundations

Missing or duplicate title tags, no meta descriptions, broken internal links, images without alt text, and a difficult crawl structure.

06
Slow Page Load & Poor Core Web Vitals

Mobile PageSpeed scores were below 40. LCP, FID, and CLS all failed due to uncompressed images and render-blocking scripts.

07
No Schema Markup

No LocalBusiness, Service, or Review schema — rich result opportunities entirely unclaimed.

08
Minimal Domain Authority

Almost no backlinks and no citation footprint meant near-zero local authority and no third-party validation for Google.

09
No Review Strategy

Fewer than five Google reviews despite 12 years of satisfied customers — a direct drag on Local Pack rankings and click-through rates.

10
No Reporting or Tracking

No analytics goals, no call tracking, and no Search Console data being monitored. No baseline to measure performance against.

SEO Strategy

The strategy was built around eight interconnected pillars, each addressing a specific gap identified during the initial audit. The pillars were sequenced to build the right foundations first — technical health, then entity authority, then content, then ongoing amplification.

📍
GBP Optimisation
  • Claim and verify the listing
  • Set primary and secondary categories
  • Write keyword-rich business description
  • Upload 20+ professional photos
  • Add all services with descriptions
  • Activate Q&A and Google Posts
  • Launch review acquisition campaign
📋
Citation Building
  • Audit existing citations for NAP consistency
  • Build 45 targeted local and niche citations
  • Prioritise high-DA UK directories
  • Include trade-specific directories
  • Ensure exact NAP match across all listings
  • Monitor indexation progress monthly
📄
Local Landing Pages
  • Create 5 city-specific service pages
  • Unique H1, H2 structure per page
  • Location-specific content and copy
  • Embed Google Map on each page
  • LocalBusiness + Service schema
  • CTA and contact form above fold
⚙️
Technical SEO
  • Fix all crawl errors in Search Console
  • Optimise title tags and meta descriptions
  • Implement canonical tags
  • Fix broken links and redirects
  • Create and submit XML sitemap
  • Ensure mobile-first indexation
⚡
Core Web Vitals
  • Compress and convert images to WebP
  • Implement lazy loading
  • Defer render-blocking scripts
  • Enable browser caching / CDN
  • Fix Cumulative Layout Shift issues
  • Target 90+ PageSpeed on mobile
🔍
Keyword Research
  • Map all service + location combinations
  • Identify emergency and high-intent terms
  • Analyse competitor ranking gaps
  • Build keyword clusters per landing page
  • Track 20+ primary keywords weekly
🔗
Internal Linking
  • Audit existing internal link structure
  • Create silo structure for service pages
  • Link landing pages to/from homepage
  • Add contextual links in body content
  • Add breadcrumbs with schema
🏆
Local Authority Building
  • Register with local business associations
  • Earn links from trade bodies and schemes
  • Google Posts published weekly
  • Respond to all reviews publicly
  • Optimise for "near me" queries
